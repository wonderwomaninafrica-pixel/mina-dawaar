The Pale Room

The heat was real. Whether he was is another question.

She has a marriage she doesn't examine too closely, an apartment she keeps very tidy, and a room she has never set foot in that visits her most nights — tall windows, cold tile, a mark on the floor she could point to with her eyes shut.

Then the messages start. Someone online who knows the room. Someone who knows things about her he has no ordinary way of knowing.

She tells herself it's a coincidence, then a small mercy, then something she isn't ready to name. In the hours no one else is awake for, the two of them build a life together — tender, explicit, exact — while her waking hours grow thinner around her.

By the time she finds the hotel the room belongs to, she is no longer sure which version of her life she is supposed to be living.
